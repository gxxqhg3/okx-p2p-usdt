## What Is OKX P2P Trading?

Peer-to-peer trading sounds fancy, but the idea is simple. Instead of buying crypto from the exchange itself, you buy it directly from another person. OKX just stands in the middle to make sure nobody gets cheated.

Here's the mechanics. A seller posts an offer: "I'll sell 1,000 USDT for 1.002 USD each, accept bank transfer." You see that offer, click buy, send the money to their bank account, and once they confirm the payment arrived, OKX releases the USDT from escrow to your wallet. The exchange never touches your fiat money — that goes directly between you and the seller. OKX only holds the crypto in escrow to guarantee the seller can't run off with your cash.

This setup matters for a few reasons. First, you're not limited to whatever price the exchange sets. You can shop around between different sellers, compare rates, and pick the one that works for you. Second, you can use payment methods that regular exchanges don't support — local bank transfers, e-wallets, even cash deposit in some regions. Third, your bank details stay private. You share them only with the specific person you're trading with, not with the platform.

OKX's P2P marketplace supports over 100 fiat currencies and more than 900 payment methods. USDT is the most traded asset there, but you can also find BTC, ETH, and USDC offers.

## Tips for Safe P2P Trading — Avoiding the Common Scams

P2P trading is safe when you follow the rules. It's dangerous when you don't. Here's what experienced traders do:

**Trade with verified merchants whenever possible.** Look for the verified badge, high trade counts (1,000+ completed trades is a good benchmark), and completion rates above 95%. A seller with 10,000 trades and 99% completion is dramatically safer than a new account with 5 trades and no history.

**Never take communication off-platform.** Scammers often try to move you to Telegram, WhatsApp, or email where OKX can't see the conversation. If there's a dispute later, OKX only reviews in-app chat logs. If you discussed the trade on Telegram, that evidence doesn't count. Keep everything in the OKX in-app chat.

**Confirm payment actually arrived before releasing crypto.** If you're selling, check your bank account directly — not just a screenshot the buyer sends you. Fake payment slips are a common scam. Log into your banking app, verify the deposit is there, and only then click "Release."

**Screenshot everything.** Payment confirmations, chat conversations, order details. If a dispute opens, you'll need evidence. The more documentation you have, the faster OKX support can resolve things in your favor.

**Start small.** Your first P2P trade should be a small amount — maybe $50-100 worth of USDT. Get comfortable with the flow. Once you understand how everything works, scale up. There's no rush, and learning on a small trade means any mistake costs you little.

**Watch for overpayment scams.** If a buyer "accidentally" sends you more than the agreed amount and asks you to refund the difference, this is almost certainly a scam. The original payment may be from a stolen account and will be reversed. Keep the full amount in escrow and open a dispute instead.
